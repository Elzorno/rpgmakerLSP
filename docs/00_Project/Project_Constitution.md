# The Last Sword Protocol — Project Constitution

> The player believes they are restoring magic.
>
> In truth, they are restoring trust.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of *The Last Sword Protocol* is to create a Dragon Quest-inspired JRPG that begins as a warm fantasy adventure and slowly reveals a hidden technological truth beneath its mythology.

The game should feel familiar to players who love classic JRPGs: villages, caves, monsters, exploration, secrets, ships, ruins, legendary weapons, and quiet moments of human kindness.

But beneath that familiar shape is an original world where magic is forgotten engineering, curses are compromise, ancient seals are access controls, relics are hardware security devices, and the final battle is not merely about destroying evil.

It is about restoring trust.

---

## The Core Identity

*The Last Sword Protocol* is not a cybersecurity training game.

It is not an AI ethics lecture.

It is not a post-apocalyptic survival game.

Those ideas exist in the world, but they are not the heart of the experience.

The heart of the experience is:

- curiosity,
- inheritance,
- responsibility,
- restoration,
- wisdom,
- hope.

Technology built the old world.

People built civilization.

The player should never mourn the loss of machines first. They should mourn the people who once used them.

Every ruin once held laughter.

Every relic was once ordinary.

Every impossible machine was once someone’s job.

---

## Immutable Principles

These principles are permanent unless the entire creative direction of the project changes.

1. **Wonder before explanation.**
2. **Curiosity is always rewarded.**
3. **Hope always survives.**
4. **There is no unexplained magic.**
5. **Every mystery has an answer, even if the player never learns it.**
6. **Technology serves people, not the other way around.**
7. **Exploration is meaningful.**
8. **Every ruin once belonged to ordinary people.**
9. **The player is intelligent and should be treated with respect.**
10. **Consistency is more important than spectacle.**
11. **The story should feel like Dragon Quest in spirit, not imitation.**

---

## The Motto

> Knowledge is power.
>
> Wisdom is the protocol.

This sentence should guide major story, gameplay, and world-building decisions.

---

## The Final Question

Most JRPGs ask:

> Can you save the world?

*The Last Sword Protocol* asks:

> What is worth preserving?

The answer is not technology alone.

It is curiosity, compassion, responsibility, wisdom, memory, and hope.

---

## Canonical Decision

The game must always present itself first as an emotionally sincere classic JRPG adventure. The hidden cybersecurity and engineering layers must deepen the world for players who notice them, but they must never overwhelm the fantasy experience.
