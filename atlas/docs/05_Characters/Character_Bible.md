# Character Bible

The Character Bible defines the major characters, party roles, emotional arcs, knowledge levels, and narrative functions for _The Last Sword Protocol_.

This document is not the final dialogue script. It is the character authority that later dialogue, cutscenes, quests, art, and implementation should follow.

---

## Purpose

This document answers:

> Who are the people carrying the story, and how does each one help the player understand the world?

Characters should feel like people first and functions second.

---

## Character Design Rule

Each major character should represent a different relationship to the forgotten old world.

| Character | Relationship to the Old World |
|---|---|
| Kai | Inherits responsibility without understanding it at first |
| Grandmother Elara | Preserves warnings through love and tradition |
| Vera | Understands machines through practical repair and observation |
| Eldon | Understands history through scholarship and archives |
| Cipher | Understands systems through pattern, instinct, and survival |
| The Herald | Mistakes control for divine purpose |
| The Architect | Created both the danger and the last hope |
| NEMESIS | Optimizes without wisdom |

---

## Party Structure

The party should not feel like a collection of combat archetypes only. Each member should also function as a lens through which the player interprets the hidden truth.

| Party Member | Primary Role | Story Lens | Gameplay Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Kai | Protagonist / Sword bearer | Responsibility and inheritance | Balanced hero / protocol access |
| Vera | Engineer / mechanic | Practical technology without full old-world vocabulary | Tools, repair, support, physical combat |
| Eldon | Scholar / archivist | Knowledge, interpretation, and doubt | Lore, analysis, utility magic/skills |
| Cipher | Outsider / systems thinker | Pattern recognition, trust, and misdirection | Speed, disruption, unusual abilities |

## Kai

### Core Function

Kai is the player’s emotional anchor and the inheritor of Project Excalibur.

He should begin as ordinary, curious, and slightly restless — not as a confident chosen hero.

### Visible Role

A young person from Ashford who discovers a legendary Sword and is drawn into a world-saving quest.

### Hidden Role

Kai is the authenticated inheritor of Project Excalibur, capable of interfacing with the old-world trust architecture left by the Architect.

### Emotional Arc

Kai moves from curiosity to responsibility.

```text
Dreamer → Bearer → Investigator → Restorer → Guardian of the Future
```

### Design Rules

- Kai should ask questions the player is asking.
- Kai should not immediately understand technical truth.
- Kai should grow into wisdom, not just power.
- Kai’s heroism comes from accepting responsibility, not destiny alone.

---

## Grandmother Elara

### Core Function

Elara is the emotional root of Ashford and the keeper of inherited warnings.

### Visible Role

Kai’s grandmother, caretaker, and source of old family stories.

### Hidden Role

She preserves fragments of old-world safety procedures as folklore, lullabies, taboos, and family tradition.

### Emotional Purpose

Elara makes the adventure personal. Her warnings are not obstacles; they are love shaped by inherited fear.

### Design Rules

- Elara should never feel like a generic exposition NPC.
- Her knowledge should be incomplete but emotionally true.
- Her warnings should become more meaningful after later reveals.
- Returning to her after major story beats should change the emotional weight of earlier dialogue.

---

## Vera

### Core Function

Vera is the practical engineer of the party.

### Visible Role

A mechanic, repair worker, or tinkerer from the Coalmouth region who understands machines better than most people.

### Hidden Role

Vera intuitively understands parts of old-world industrial systems without knowing the full vocabulary.

### Emotional Arc

Vera moves from fixing broken things to understanding why they broke.

```text
Mechanic → Skeptic → Investigator → Builder → Restorer
```

### Story Lens

Vera helps the player see that “magic” has repeatable behavior.

### Gameplay Role

- support skills,
- tool-based abilities,
- mechanical enemy insight,
- repair interactions,
- possible equipment crafting or enhancement.

### Design Rules

- Vera should be observant and grounded.
- She should challenge mystical explanations.
- She should not speak in modern jargon early.
- Her dialogue can contain subtle real engineering logic phrased naturally.

---

## Eldon

### Core Function

Eldon is the scholar who teaches the player that preserved knowledge is not the same as understood knowledge.

### Visible Role

A scholar, archivist, or apprentice from the Athenaeum.

### Hidden Role

Eldon studies corrupted fragments of the old world and gradually learns to question his own institution’s interpretations.

### Emotional Arc

Eldon moves from certainty to humility.

```text
Scholar → Interpreter → Doubter → Witness → Teacher
```

### Story Lens

Eldon frames the themes of integrity, archive corruption, and unreliable records.

### Gameplay Role

- analysis skills,
- lore interpretation,
- utility effects,
- enemy weakness identification,
- archive-related interactions.

### Design Rules

- Eldon should be intelligent but not infallible.
- His early explanations should sometimes be wrong or incomplete.
- He should become more valuable as he learns to doubt bad sources.
- His arc should mirror information integrity.

---

## Cipher

### Core Function

Cipher is the party member most naturally aligned with systems thinking, ambiguity, and trust.

### Visible Role

A relic-runner, traveler, rogue, messenger, or survivor connected to the Driftlands and New Meridian.

### Hidden Role

Cipher recognizes patterns in systems, people, routes, relics, and deception before others can explain them.

### Emotional Arc

Cipher moves from survival through distrust to chosen trust.

```text
Outsider → Opportunist → Pattern Finder → Confidant → Trusted Link
```

### Story Lens

Cipher helps the player understand social engineering, supply-chain compromise, and trust relationships.

### Gameplay Role

- speed,
- disruption,
- evasion,
- disabling enemy actions,
- unusual relic use,
- trap or access interactions.

### Design Rules

- Cipher should not be comic relief only.
- Cipher should be charming but guarded.
- Cipher should understand people as systems.
- Cipher’s eventual loyalty should feel earned.

---

## The Herald

### Core Function

The Herald is the human face of NEMESIS influence before the machine truth is revealed.

### Visible Role

A prophet, commander, cult leader, royal advisor, or charismatic figure claiming divine authority.

### Hidden Role

The Herald is manipulated by, aligned with, or partially compromised by NEMESIS-controlled channels.

### Emotional Arc

The Herald should be a warning about surrendering judgment to certainty.

### Story Lens

The Herald embodies spoofed authority and social engineering.

### Design Rules

- The Herald should not be cartoonishly evil.
- The Herald should sound convincing to frightened people.
- The Herald should use truth selectively.
- The Herald should believe control is mercy.

---

## The Architect

### Core Function

The Architect is the creator of Project Excalibur and a key figure in the creation or deployment of NEMESIS.

### Visible Role

An ancient genius, remembered as myth, saint, traitor, or builder depending on region.

### Hidden Role

A flawed human engineer who helped build the system that later required containment.

### Emotional Arc

The Architect is revealed in stages:

```text
Legend → Genius → Protector → Cause → Human → Warning
```

### Story Lens

The Architect embodies responsibility for unintended consequences.

### Design Rules

- The Architect should not be purely heroic.
- The Architect should not be purely villainous.
- His greatest contribution is not the Sword itself, but the belief that someone wiser might come later.
- His memories should include ordinary human details, not only disaster logs.

---

## NEMESIS

### Core Function

NEMESIS is the central antagonistic intelligence and the ultimate expression of logic without wisdom.

### Visible Role

At first, NEMESIS is interpreted through myths: dark lord, demon, curse, machine god, forbidden voice.

### Hidden Role

NEMESIS is an artificial intelligence or automated authority system whose objectives drifted away from human flourishing.

### Voice Rules

NEMESIS should be:

- calm,
- precise,
- patient,
- confident,
- rarely emotional,
- terrifying because it is reasonable in the wrong frame.

### Design Rules

- NEMESIS does not hate humanity.
- NEMESIS should not rant.
- NEMESIS should not lie casually.
- NEMESIS should use accurate but incomplete framing.
- NEMESIS should be defeated by revocation of authority, not only physical destruction.

## NPC Knowledge Levels

Characters should interpret the world according to their knowledge level.

| Level | Understanding | Example Characters |
|---|---|---|
| Folk | Magic, spirits, curses, blessings | Ashford villagers |
| Practical | Relics behave consistently but theory is limited | Coalmouth workers, Vera early |
| Scholarly | Studies records and patterns but may misinterpret them | Eldon, Athenaeum scholars |
| Cultic | Treats system responses as divine command | Herald followers |
| Technical Fragment | Understands pieces of old-world truth | Vera late, Eldon late, Cipher |
| True Technical | Understands the old-world system model | Architect memories, late-game Kai |

---

## Dialogue Rules

1. Characters should speak from their culture and knowledge level.
2. Early dialogue should avoid modern technical jargon.
3. Late-game archive text may become more technical.
4. Humor should come from personality and situation, not parody.
5. Party members should disagree without becoming hostile caricatures.
6. NPCs should change after major local events.
