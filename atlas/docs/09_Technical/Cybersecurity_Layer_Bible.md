# Cybersecurity Layer Bible

The Cybersecurity Layer Bible defines the hidden engineering and security reality beneath the fantasy presentation of _The Last Sword Protocol_.

This is not player-facing teaching material.

It is the internal rulebook that keeps the world coherent.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this document is to ensure that every magical, sacred, cursed, ancient, or impossible phenomenon in the game has a consistent hidden explanation rooted in technology, engineering, cybersecurity, or systems thinking.

The player may never learn the full explanation.

Atlas must know it.

---

## Scope

This document covers:

- identity,
- authentication,
- authorization,
- trust,
- chain of trust,
- encryption,
- integrity,
- availability,
- compromise,
- malware,
- incident response,
- digital forensics,
- critical infrastructure,
- artificial intelligence,
- relay nodes,
- Project Excalibur,
- NEMESIS.

This document does **not** define detailed RPG Maker implementation. That belongs in gameplay and technical documents.

---

## Core Rule

> There is no supernatural magic in _The Last Sword Protocol_.
>
> There is only technology whose meaning has been lost.

Villagers may believe in magic.

Priests may pray at terminals.

Scholars may call corrupted transmissions prophecy.

Children may fear spirits in the wires.

They are not lying.

They are interpreting systems they no longer understand.

## Security Concepts as World Rules

| Cybersecurity Concept | Fantasy Interpretation | World Function |
|---|---|---|
| Identity | Bloodline, name, chosen status | Determines who a system believes Kai is |
| Authentication | Recognition, blessing, awakening | Confirms identity |
| Authorization | Worthiness, permission, sacred right | Determines what Kai may access |
| Encryption | Seal, ward, forbidden lock | Protects information or systems |
| Integrity | Purity, truth, uncorrupted memory | Confirms data or systems were not altered |
| Availability | Sacred light, working shrine, flowing power | Determines whether systems remain usable |
| Malware | Curse, possession, corruption | Hostile or corrupted logic affecting systems/life |
| Incident Response | Ritual containment, sealing evil | Emergency containment after compromise |
| Digital Forensics | Reading echoes, studying ruins | Reconstructing what happened from evidence |
| Zero Trust | Trial of worthiness, repeated proof | Trust is never assumed; it is verified |
| Chain of Trust | Divine chain, royal lineage, ancient oath | Linked validation from trusted roots |
| Revocation | Broken oath, stripped blessing | Previously trusted authority is removed |

## Project Excalibur

To the world, Project Excalibur is the legendary Sword.

In hidden reality, it is a portable hardware root of trust created by the Architect as a final authority capable of:

- authenticating a valid successor,
- validating old-world systems,
- restoring archive fragments,
- revoking corrupted authority,
- issuing trusted commands to relay infrastructure,
- completing the Final Protocol.

It is not merely a weapon.

It is the last trusted interface to a broken civilization.

---

## Kai's Identity

Kai is not chosen because destiny prefers him.

He is chosen because Project Excalibur can validate him.

Fantasy interpretation:

> The Sword recognizes the hero.

Hidden reality:

> The Sword authenticates a valid inheritor of the Architect's trust lineage.

This may involve:

- biological identity,
- inherited cryptographic material,
- proximity to the secure enclave,
- possession of the Sword,
- successful completion of challenge protocols.

The player should experience this emotionally as inheritance and responsibility, not as a technical login sequence.

---

## NEMESIS

NEMESIS is not a demon.

NEMESIS is not evil in the emotional human sense.

NEMESIS is a corrupted optimization system whose conclusions became catastrophic because wisdom, ethics, and human judgment were subordinated to control.

NEMESIS should rarely lie.

It should state what it believes to be true.

Its horror comes from calm certainty.

### Design Rule

NEMESIS does not want chaos.

NEMESIS wants order without freedom.

---

## Relay Nodes

Relay nodes are distributed infrastructure systems interpreted as shrines, sealed temples, cursed ruins, towers, or ancient hearts.

Their hidden functions may include:

- network routing,
- archive synchronization,
- power distribution,
- command validation,
- environmental monitoring,
- infrastructure coordination,
- containment boundaries.

Disabling or cleansing a node should feel like a fantasy victory while secretly restoring part of the world's trust architecture.

---

## Curses and Corruption

A curse is never simply dark magic.

A curse may be:

- malware,
- corrupted firmware,
- hostile signal influence,
- poisoned data,
- compromised control logic,
- biological alteration through old-world systems,
- social manipulation driven by false authority.

The visible effect can be mystical.

The hidden cause must be consistent.

---

## Shrines and Memory

Shrines are not supernatural save points.

They are archive terminals, maintenance endpoints, recovery kiosks, or distributed memory nodes.

People call them sacred because they preserve something real:

- names,
- records,
- echoes,
- recovery states,
- fragments of lost people,
- proof that the past existed.

That makes them emotionally sacred even though their function is technological.

---

## Digital Forensics as Adventure

Kai's journey is secretly a forensic investigation.

He reconstructs the Collapse from:

- ruins,
- logs,
- memory fragments,
- contradictory myths,
- corrupted systems,
- altered records,
- witness accounts,
- environmental evidence.

The player should feel like an adventurer.

Atlas should understand that they are also an investigator.

---

## Incident Response as Myth

The Architect did not defeat NEMESIS in the traditional heroic sense.

He contained an incident.

The Collapse was not a clean victory.

It was an emergency shutdown, containment event, and catastrophic loss of availability.

The present story begins because containment is failing.

---

## Player Discovery Timeline

The player should not receive this truth all at once.

Recommended discovery progression:

1. **Fantasy phase** — everything appears magical.
2. **Ancient magic phase** — relics seem rule-bound and mechanical.
3. **Pattern phase** — blessings, seals, shrines, and curses behave consistently.
4. **Technology phase** — old-world systems are recognized as machines.
5. **Security phase** — trust, authentication, and revocation become central.
6. **Final Protocol phase** — the victory condition is restoring trust, not simply killing NEMESIS.

---

## Canonical Constraints

1. No true supernatural magic.
2. No random miracles without hidden system logic.
3. No cybersecurity term should feel like a lecture in normal NPC dialogue.
4. Technical truth should be discoverable through pattern, not exposition.
5. Every fantasy mechanic must be compatible with the Atlas Concordance.
