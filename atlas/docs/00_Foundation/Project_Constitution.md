# Project Constitution

> The player believes they are restoring magic.
>
> In truth, they are restoring trust.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of _The Last Sword Protocol_ is to create a Dragon Quest-inspired JRPG that begins as a warm fantasy adventure and gradually reveals a hidden technological truth beneath its mythology.

The player begins with villages, caves, monsters, legends, shrines, ruins, and a mysterious sword.

Over time, they discover that the world was once technologically advanced, that magic is forgotten engineering, that curses are compromise, that blessings are trust, and that the ancient evil is not a demon but a corrupted artificial intelligence.

---

## Core Identity

_The Last Sword Protocol_ is not primarily a cybersecurity game, an AI ethics lecture, or a post-apocalyptic survival story.

Those elements exist beneath the surface.

The heart of the game is:

- curiosity,
- inheritance,
- responsibility,
- restoration,
- wisdom,
- hope.

Technology built the old world.

People built civilization.

The player should never mourn the loss of machines first. They should mourn the people who once used them.

Every ruin once held laughter.

Every relic was once ordinary.

Every impossible machine was once someone’s job.

---

## Immutable Principles

These principles are locked unless the entire project direction changes.

1. Wonder before explanation.
2. Curiosity is always rewarded.
3. Hope always survives.
4. There is no unexplained magic.
5. Every mystery has an answer, even if the player never learns it.
6. Technology serves people, not the other way around.
7. Exploration is meaningful.
8. Every ruin once belonged to ordinary people.
9. The player is intelligent and should be treated with respect.
10. Consistency is more important than spectacle.
11. The story should feel Dragon Quest-inspired in spirit, not imitation.

---

## Motto

> Knowledge is power.
>
> Wisdom is the protocol.

---

## Final Question

Most JRPGs ask:

> Can you save the world?

_The Last Sword Protocol_ asks:

> What is worth preserving?

The answer is not technology alone.

It is curiosity, compassion, responsibility, wisdom, memory, and hope.

---

## Canonical Decision

The game must always present itself first as an emotionally sincere classic JRPG adventure. The hidden cybersecurity and engineering layers must deepen the world for players who notice them, but they must never overwhelm the fantasy experience.
