# DDR-0002 — No Supernatural Magic

## Status

Locked

---

## Context

_The Last Sword Protocol_ presents itself as a fantasy JRPG with swords, magic, shrines, monsters, blessings, curses, and ancient ruins.

However, the project's unique identity depends on a hidden engineering and cybersecurity layer beneath the fantasy presentation.

---

## Decision

There is no true supernatural magic in the setting.

All magic-like phenomena have a technological, biological, social, or engineered explanation known to Atlas.

The player does not need to learn every explanation.

---

## Rationale

This preserves internal consistency and allows the fantasy layer to map cleanly to forgotten technology and cybersecurity concepts.

It also supports the core revelation: the world did not lose magic; it forgot engineering.

---

## Consequences

- Writers may use fantasy language in-world.
- NPCs may believe in spirits, blessings, curses, divine relics, and magic.
- Atlas must define the hidden reality beneath those beliefs.
- New systems should not introduce unexplained supernatural exceptions.
- If something appears magical, the design team must know what it really is.

---
